Mapo Tofu

Description

Mapo tofu is a spicy, savory Chinese dish made with tofu in a sauce that typically includes ground pork, fermented bean paste, soy sauce, and garlic. Itâ€™s known for its bold, complex flavor profile, with a balance of heat from chili peppers and a numbing sensation from Sichuan peppercorns. The dish is often served with steamed rice.

    Ingredients
  • Ground pork 100g
  • Firm Tofu 1 box
  • Ginger 10g
  • Scallion 20g
  • Hondashi 200ml
  • Doubanjiang (spicy fermented bean paste) 3 tablespoons
  • Spicy Oil 1 tablespoon
  • Rice Wine 1 tablespoon
  • Sichuan Peppercorn Powder 1 teaspoon
  • Sichuan Peppercorn Oil 2 tablespoons
    Steps
  1. Cut the tofu in to 1 inch cubes. Add to a saucepan and boil with water for 5 minutes. Drain and set aside
  2. Head large skillet on high heat
  3. Stir fry ground pork until browned and crispy
  4. Add Doubanjiang, spicy oil, ginger, garlic and hondashi. Let simmer for a few minutes.
  5. Lower heat to medium. Add tofu and soy sauce in to pot. Combine
  6. Add Sichuan Peppercorn oil, Sichuan Peppercorn powder. Mix until combined. Then garnish with scallions and serve over rice.
